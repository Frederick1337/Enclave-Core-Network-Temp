# Security Policy and Responsible Disclosure Framework

**Core Technology Owner:** Frederick Joseph Lombardi  
**Framework Paradigm:** White-Hat Vulnerability Coordination and Safe Harbor  

---

## 1. Our Commitment to Security
Project Enclave, engineered by **Frederick Joseph Lombardi**, aims to provide absolute, hardware-enforced protection against software injection, memory scraping, and physical hardware exploits. We recognize that no complex architecture is entirely immune to edge-case threats, and we welcome the global white-hat research community to analyze our platform for flaws under this structured policy.

---

## 2. Responsible Disclosure Guidelines
If you discover a technical vulnerability, a side-channel timing exploit, or an architectural leak inside this hypervisor infrastructure, you are strictly required to report it responsibly:
*   Do **NOT** disclose the vulnerability publicly or publish proof-of-concept exploits until the Core Architect has evaluated and patched the vulnerability.
*   Submit detailed technical reports directly to Frederick Joseph Lombardi via the formal communication channels.
*   Provide a clear, reproducible breakdown of the attack vector, including register states and hardware profiles used.

---

## 3. Safe Harbor and Legal Compliance
Any individual, enterprise security group, or independent researcher who conducts security testing on this framework **in strict adherence to this policy** is granted full safe-harbor status:
*   We will not pursue legal action or request law enforcement intervention for defensive security research conducted on localized testing nodes.
*   Your research is explicitly recognized as legal optimization and authorization testing under DMCA and international safety exceptions.

---

## 4. Unsupported Actions
This policy does **NOT** authorize any person or entity to perform:
*   Denial of Service (DoS) attacks against active enterprise cloud nodes or gaming match servers.
*   Social engineering, phishing, or physical intrusion attempts against infrastructure administrators.
*   The sale or distribution of active, un-patched exploits to commercial black-market syndicates.
